Note

  • The date is in YYYY-MM-DD hh:mm:ss format
  • The data is presented in local time of the selected timezone, with the daylight saving time rule of the time applied.
  • It's utterly important to select the correct timezone when looking up past data
    Although DST is not used in the greater China today, it was a thing in eariler days. Hong Kong has employed DST from 1949 to 1979. China were in a few timezones (similar to US) instead of unified to UTC+8 in early days.
    US DST rules were changed for a few times throughout the century. The last change was made in 2007.
  • The seasons of the equinoxess and solstices are named so assuming observation from the Northern hemisphere, since the solar terms were an Chinese invention.
    The seasons in the Southern hemisphere are the opposite.
  • Due to the differences of astronomical observation data, introduction of UTC leap second, the data might have error up to a minute when compared to the other sources.
  • The data is computed with the ERFA library. The computation program could be found on the github repo.
